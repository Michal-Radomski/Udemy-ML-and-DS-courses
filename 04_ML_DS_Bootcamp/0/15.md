CPU, GPU, NPU, and TPU are specialized processors designed for different computing needs, with CPUs handling general tasks
and the others optimized for parallel or AI workloads.
[eitc](http://eitc.org/research-opportunities/new-media-and-new-digital-economy/future-compute-and-microelectronics/cpu-vs-gpu-vs-tpu-vs-npu)

## Core Functions

CPUs (Central Processing Units) manage sequential tasks like running operating systems, office apps, and logic operations
across a few powerful cores. [ibm](https://www.ibm.com/think/topics/npu-vs-gpu) GPUs (Graphics Processing Units) excel at
parallel processing for graphics, gaming, video editing, and training neural networks with thousands of smaller cores.
[linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aditya-hicounselor_aichips-gpu-tpu-activity-7382399548519895041-7ACA) NPUs (Neural
Processing Units) accelerate AI inference on devices like smartphones, mimicking brain-like data flow for efficient,
low-power tasks such as face recognition.
[eitc](http://eitc.org/research-opportunities/new-media-and-new-digital-economy/future-compute-and-microelectronics/cpu-vs-gpu-vs-tpu-vs-npu)
TPUs (Tensor Processing Units), Google's custom ASICs, specialize in tensor math for TensorFlow-based machine learning
training at scale.
[linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aditya-hicounselor_aichips-gpu-tpu-activity-7382399548519895041-7ACA)
